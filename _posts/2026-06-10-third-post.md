This is another way I put pen to paper during my first few years of college. I was introduced to D&D and TTRPGs pretty late in life, but I've quickly fallen completely in love. Despite being quite new, I'm not above criticizing D&D itself and looking for ways to improve it. This particular draft was intended to add a bit more juice to one of my favorite character archetypes, the Artificer.


There is a huge conversation in the design space of TTRPGs that lies in the undercurrent of any change like this: is it better to have more rules or less rules. The ins and outs of both sides is too much to cover here (I'll leave that to a future blog post) but suffice to say at the time I subscribed to the more rules camp. Nowadays, I hold a more nuanced view, which means that I look back on this project with a more critical eye as to its fundamentals. However, I still believe it to have been a valuable piece of design practice uncluttered by the typical trappings of the video game format.
