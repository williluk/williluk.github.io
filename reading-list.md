Games have always been quintessential to my life. Here are a few that affected me the most:

1. Disco Elysium: The Final Cut - *ZA/UM*

    Legendary amongst those who value games as artistic pieces. Profound writing, beautiful art, and gameplay that subtly sets the mood.

2. Sorry We're Closed - *À la Mode Games*

   Love a classic subject for art, but nothing that I've seen addresses the terror of love quite like this game.

3. Elden Ring - *From Software*

   Certainly mainstream, but From never fails to remind me how to manifest the willpower and patience that is needed to face life's challenges.  

4. Spec Ops: The Line - *Yager*

   Soul crushing and terrifying in ways only a game can be.

5. The Alters - *11 Bit*

   Finally, something joyful. A fantastic management game that doesn't shy away from a relatable and engaging human narrative.

6. DOOM (2016) - *id Software*

   A simple and yet potent example of the raw emotive power that mechanics can evoke. Must play to understand the basics of my design philosophy.
